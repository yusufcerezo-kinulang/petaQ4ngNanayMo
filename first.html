Editor's Note

Social media is a big part of teenagers’ lives today because it helps them communicate with friends and share their thoughts online. Many teens use platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook to connect with others and express themselves. It can also help them find support from people who have similar interests or experiences. However, social media also has some negative effects if it is used too much. It can distract teenagers from schoolwork, sleep, and family time, and sometimes it exposes them to cyberbullying or false information.

Another problem is that teenagers often compare themselves to the “perfect” images they see online. This can lower their self-esteem and make them feel anxious or lonely. Experts say that too much time on social media can also affect teens’ mental health and social skills because they spend less time communicating face-to-face with others. Because of this, teenagers should learn how to use social media responsibly and balance their time online and offline.

Experts also explain that the content teenagers see online is not always realistic. Many photos and videos are edited or carefully chosen to look perfect, which can make teens compare their lives with others. When this happens, some teenagers may feel unhappy with themselves or their achievements. This is why it is important for teenagers to use social media in a healthy way, limit their screen time, and focus on real-life relationships and activities. By balancing online and offline life, teens can enjoy the benefits of social media while protecting their mental health.
